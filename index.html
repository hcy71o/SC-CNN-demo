SC-CNN-demo: "An Effective Style Conditioning Method for Zero-Shot Text-to-Speech System"

Authors: Hyungchan Yoon, Seyun Um, Chanhwan Kim, Hong-Goo Kang

Code

Abstract

This letter proposes an efficient style/speaker- conditioning method that is applicable to zero-shot text-to-speech (TTS) systems. Based on the inductive bias in the speech generation task, i.e. local context information in text/phoneme sequences heavily affect the style of the output speech, we propose a Style-Conditional Convolutional Neural Network (SC-CNN). Our proposed SC-CNN first predicts convolutional kernels from each learned style representation, then applies 1-D convolution to phoneme sequences with the predicted kernels. It successfully utilizes the above inductive bias and effectively provides the style- specific local dependencies to the phoneme hidden states. We design SC-CNN for transformer encoder, and adopt SC-CNN and other conventional conditioning methods to FastSpeech 2-based system under the same setup for experiments. The results confirm that SC-CNN outperforms the conventional methods which have been used in recent few- or zero-shot TTS models in terms of both subjective and objective evaluations.
